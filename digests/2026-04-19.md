# Daily Inspiration Digest — 2026-04-19

---

## Dev Tools

### [Sostactic – Polynomial Inequalities via Sums-of-Squares in Lean](https://github.com/mmaaz-git/sostactic)

A Lean 4 tactic that automatically proves polynomial inequalities by decomposing them into sums-of-squares certificates. Formal verification of inequalities is notoriously tedious—this collapses hours of manual proof work into a single tactic call. **Side project angle:** Build automated tactics for other painful proof categories in Lean (combinatorial identities, matrix inequalities) and you'll have a grateful audience in the growing formal methods community.

---

## Consumer

### [Faceoff – A Terminal UI for Following NHL Games](https://www.vincentgregoire.com/faceoff/)

A lightweight TUI that streams live NHL scores, stats, and game flow straight to your terminal. Developers who refuse to leave the command line finally get a distraction-free sports ticker that fits their workflow. **Side project angle:** Clone this pattern for any sport or live data feed—F1 telemetry, soccer, cricket, even esports. The "TUI for X" niche is underserved and builds fast (1–2 weeks).

## Wild Cards

### [Google Gemini Is Scanning Your Photos – and the EU Said No](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47825520)

Google Gemini is processing users' photos by default; EU regulators stepped in to block it. Most people have no idea their personal images are training or being analyzed by AI models behind the scenes. **Side project angle:** Build a local-first photo audit tool that flags which of your images have been sent to cloud AI services, across Google, Apple, and others. Privacy-aware tooling is a wide-open space—especially anything that makes opaque data flows visible with a single scan.

---

*Three items, three directions: formal math automation, terminal-native live data, and privacy tooling. Pick the one that makes you angry or curious—that's the one worth building.*
