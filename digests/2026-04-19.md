## Dev Tools

**MDV — Markdown superset for docs, dashboards, and slides**
A single Markdown-like language that outputs documents, dashboards, and slide decks with live embedded data. Kills the tool-switching tax when your content needs charts alongside prose.
→ [github.com/drasimwagan/mdv](https://github.com/drasimwagan/mdv)

**T3Code — A code editor purpose-built for the T3 stack**
Ping.gg is building a dev environment tuned specifically for T3 (Next.js, tRPC, Prisma, Tailwind) workflows. Worth studying how an opinionated editor can reduce friction for a specific ecosystem — and whether that model ports to other stacks.
→ [github.com/pingdotgg/t3code](https://github.com/pingdotgg/t3code)

## Productivity

**Solyto — Open-source all-in-one personal management**
Tasks, notes, habits, and personal org in a single free app instead of five subscriptions. The "all-in-one vs. best-of-breed" pendulum is swinging back toward consolidation — and open source gives it staying power.
→ [solyto.app](https://solyto.app)

**ARC-Kit — AI-driven enterprise architecture governance**
Automates the painful manual work of enterprise architecture reviews and vendor procurement analysis. Niche but lucrative — enterprise tooling with AI augmentation is underserved and high-margin.
→ [github.com/tractorjuice/arc-kit](https://github.com/tractorjuice/arc-kit)

**Paperless-ngx — Community document management with OCR**
Scan, index, tag, and archive all your paper and digital documents in a self-hosted, searchable system. A mature project (Python/Django/Postgres/Docker) and a great reference architecture if you want to build any document-heavy side project.
→ [github.com/paperless-ngx/paperless-ngx](https://github.com/paperless-ngx/paperless-ngx)

## Data

**FinceptTerminal — Bloomberg Terminal for the rest of us**
A Python-based terminal app offering market analytics, investment research, and economic data exploration — free and open. Retail finance tooling keeps growing; there's room to build specialized modules (crypto, commodities, ESG) on top of a project like this.
→ [github.com/Fincept-Corporation/FinceptTerminal](https://github.com/Fincept-Corporation/FinceptTerminal)

## AI/ML

**Thunderbolt — Vendor-agnostic AI assistant from Thunderbird**
An AI assistant platform where users pick their own models and retain full data ownership. The anti-lock-in angle is increasingly compelling as people grow wary of handing everything to a single provider.
→ [github.com/thunderbird/thunderbolt](https://github.com/thunderbird/thunderbolt)

**Claude-Code-Game-Studios — 49 AI agents simulating a game studio**
Wraps Claude Code in a hierarchy of 49 specialized agents (art director, QA lead, sound designer…) with 72 workflow skills to mimic a real studio pipeline. A fascinating experiment in multi-agent coordination — and shippable in a week or two.
→ [github.com/Donchitos/Claude-Code-Game-Studios](https://github.com/Donchitos/Claude-Code-Game-Studios)

## Wild Cards 🃏

**RuView — Human pose estimation and vital signs via WiFi signals**
Uses commodity WiFi channel state information to detect human presence, estimate body pose, and monitor vital signs — no cameras, no wearables, no wires. Privacy-preserving health monitoring through walls using hardware you already own. The underlying tech (WiFi CSI + DensePose) is genuinely surprising and opens doors for elderly care, smart homes, and security applications that would otherwise require invasive sensor setups.
→ [github.com/ruvnet/RuView](https://github.com/ruvnet/RuView)
